Our Genie
      Traditionally, genies are associated with intricately carved bronze oil lamps. Originating from Arab legends, genies often make us think of ancient spirits and dark mysterious caves. To others, however, genies conjure up an image of a certain cartoon and jovial Disney character.
     No one would have thought that our genie would be inside a small plastic red lamp from IKEA.
     It was a pleasant June afternoon- one of those picturesque summer days, complete with lazily drifting dandelion puffs and cloudless skies. We strolled aimlessly into IKEA, desperate for a break from the shining sun. Skipping through the aisles, we explored the nooks and crannies of the giant Swedish furniture store. After plopping onto countless couches and rearranging countless cabinets, we finally neared the �As-is� section, a place for rejected or broken goods. Rows of tables, couches and chairs greeted us, each one with its own abnormality. Some had tan dirt marks, others were missing a leg or a drawer. We perused through the rows until stumbling upon a dusty little red lamp with a torn yellow tag. "Won�t Turn On - Needs Repairs. $3". For some mysterious reason, we decided to buy this tiny lamp.
     When we got home, we examined this curious plastic anomaly. Just as we expected, when we plugged it in and flicked the switch, nothing happened. We unplugged the lamp and I sighed, sadky brushing the dust off of its light.
     Suddenly the lamp flickered on, whirring and clicking. The light seemed to swirl like golden dust. Slowly, the golden light formed into a shimmering ball.
     �Thank you for freeing me- you may have three wishes,� the ball boomed, its voice filling the room.
     Clearly, our genie was not a traditional genie, it had an updated lamp and a refreshing new appearance. We had found a modern genie.
