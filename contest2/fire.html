Surviving the Wilderness - The Can
 Soon the skies would darken into a breathtaking shade of purple that faded into lovely pinks and oranges. As much as we appreciated that view, we knew that it was time to make a fire or face the fury of nighttime chill and darkness. Much to our dismay, neither of us had worn glasses that day (we didn�t want to lose them hundreds of feet up in the air) and we hadn�t packed any beverages with glass containers for lunch either.
 Still, we managed to make do with what we had with a stroke of survival ingenuity and the contents of our wicker basket. Using a bar of chocolate that remained from our lunch (giving up chocolate was hard but we did what we had to), we scrubbed down the convex bottom of a pop can until it was gleaming. Then, reflecting the last few beams of sun for the day, we managed to spark a fire that would keep us toasty through the night.
Survival Tips
  • Build a tinder of dry grass, leaves, bark, and other materials that catch fire easily.
  • If you have one, focus sunlight through a lens start a fire, adding water to the lens can help intensify the beam.
  • Polishing the bottom of a coke can with a bar of chocolate or some toothpaste can create a reflective mirror to focus light off of.
  • Quartzite and a pocket knife can be substituted for the traditional flint and steel combo.
  • Don�t forget, fire is good for light, warmth, boiling water, and cooking.
