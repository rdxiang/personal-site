Surviving the Wilderness
  Tired after what felt like an eternity of walking in circles, and wondering how Bear Grylls made it look so easy on TV, we sat down on the ground to rest. Suddenly, we spotted a glimmer of light in the distance. Naturally distracted by sparkly objects, we began to walk over to the supernatural sparkle. To our puzzlement, we only discovered what seemed to be a rusty old red lamp. Curious, we did the sensible thing: we poked it with a stick before carefully brushing the dust off. In an instant, we were blinded by a flash of light, and in the distance a faint almost genie-like voice seemed to whisper, "You again? Be careful while 'traveling the world' - but I'll help you out this time."
  Before we could shake off this strange sense of deja-vu, a thunderous roar drowned out our thoughts. The voice was gone, but a helicopter had seen the light! Finally, it was time to go home.
Rescue at last! We won the Hunger Games!

Survival Tips

~ On a sunny day, a mirror is your best signaling device. If you don't have a mirror, polish your canteen cup, your belt buckle, or a similar object that will reflect the sun's rays.
~ During the night, a fire's probably your best bet! Make sure you have a nice open area though, you don't want your signal fire to become a forest fire (Remember Smokey the Bear)!
~ Be sure to tell people where you are going, and when you plan on returning so if you do go missing, someone will know!
