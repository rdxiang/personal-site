Surviving the Wilderness - The Knife
 Being the pragmatic survivalist we are, we knew we had to set up a shelter for the night or risk hypothermia or worse, get eaten by a bear. Luckily we had brought some utensils along with our lunch and had a knife on hand that we used to cut off some rope and a large piece of tarp from our deflated air balloon. Then, using the guidelines of BLISS (see survival tips below), we found a secluded meadow where we set-up an a-frame tent against a sturdy tree. Once again, our knife proved its worth in making tent supports as we used it to saw through a loose tree branch.
 Unfortunately, we werenï¿½t quite able to get through it using just our regular-old kitchen knife and we sat there pondering what to do. To make matters worse, a towering grizzly bear soon found its way into our clearing (so much for secluded). Petrified by fear, we stood like deer in headlights as the bear slowly stalked towards us. Our lives flashed before our eyes as the bear rose its voluminous paw in the air, ready to swipe, and we squeezed our eyes shut tight. CRACK! The sound of the tree branch being torn from its trunk startled us as we looked up in time to see the bear lumbering away.
Carlos the Bear

Survival Tips
When choosing a shelter use BLISS
  • (B)lend in with the surroundings.
  • (L)ow silhouette.
  • (I)rregular shape.
  • (S)mall.
  • (S)ecluded location.

    • And don't forget:
  • If making a lean-to, make sure to make them facing away from the wind.
  • Trees are great starting places to build shelters off of.
